# What's New in Tensies

Pull up a stool. Newest stuff up top.

## 1.6.0 ("High Roller")

Sunday, May 31, 2026

- The whole app got a warm makeover. Wood, leather, amber light across every screen.
- The landing page is cleaner and the buttons actually say what they do.
- Joined players have their own "Fellow Bar Rats" section in the lobby now.
- The player list scrolls on its own, and Start Game stays pinned so it's always there when you need it.
- The Back button became a leather pill chip. It was basically invisible before.
- Dice are more realistic, lit to match the bar background.
- Win a round and you get a moment: the dice fly toward you in 3D, hover and glow, your name appears in warm gold, and a countdown starts for the next round.
- Lose one and two cracked dice show up to let you know.
- There's a menu button on the landing, join, and lobby screens now. Quick about blurb, plus a Buy Me a Beer link for the good nights.
- The Send Message invite button got a solid icon.
- _Behind the scenes: we swapped in a self-hosted font so everything looks consistent no matter whose phone you're on._

## 1.5.0 ("Last Call")

Friday, May 30, 2026

- The host can pause now. Bar run, bathroom break, whoever's buying the next one.
- Nobody gets dropped while the game's paused. Come back and you're still in.
- The game holds for up to an hour, so a dead phone won't end the night.
- Reconnecting requires a token now. Your seat is yours, not whoever wanders back first.
- _Behind the scenes: better dashboards help us see how each game plays out, so things keep getting fairer._

## 1.4.0 ("Happy Hour")

Thursday, May 29, 2026

- The in-game menu is open. Tap the hamburger to get to game options.
- Reconnect window doubled to a full minute. The game waits for you.
- Screen transitions are a bit smoother.
- _Behind the scenes: we stress-tested multiplayer with a crowd and fixed what broke._

## 1.3.0 ("On the Rocks")

Wednesday, May 28, 2026

- Your dice remember where they landed. Refresh or reconnect and they're right where you left them.
- The loading screen is one smooth experience now, not a pile of overlapping dialogs.
- Twice as many random name options when you join.
- Fixed the winner overlay getting stuck when a late roll sneaked in during the celebration.
- Fixed error messages showing up on the wrong screen.
- _Behind the scenes: we rebuilt the codebase structure and started tracking every roll. Games should get more reliable from here._

## 1.2.0 ("Hold My Drink")

Tuesday, May 27, 2026

- Lose your connection? You've got 30 seconds to come back. You'll land on a reconnecting screen and pick up right where you left off.
- The game link shows a real preview when you share it now.
- Fixed a freeze when your re-roll landed on the exact same numbers as before. Spooky little bug.
- Fixed dice tearing mid-animation.
- Fixed join errors showing up on the wrong screen.
- The header got a dice mark, the text warmed up, and progress bars animate live now.

## 1.1.0 ("House Rules")

Monday, May 26, 2026

- Tensies has its own logo and favicon now. A red die, obviously.
- Rolls are decided by the server, not your phone. No hardware advantage.

## 1.0.0 ("Opening Tab")

Sunday, May 25, 2026

- A bar regular and his friends love playing Tensies. One night, a few rounds deep, he figured the game should work anywhere, even when you forget the dice. So he started building it with Claude, sketched the board himself, and has been tinkering from his barstool ever since.
- The dice gather up, shake, and scatter with a smooth face reveal.
- Your opponent's progress bar is right there in front of you. The trash talk writes itself.
- Invite friends by link or text. One tap and they're in.
- Works on iOS. No accidental zoom, no scroll hijack.
- Warm wood background, up to five players.
- _Behind the scenes: the git history starts here because he forgot to run git init until the game already worked._
